LATTE ART BASICS

HOW DOES LATTE ART WORK?


Latte art has become the staple of any good barsita and nowadays if you order an espresso with steamed milk in a coffee shop you'll likely find yourself marveling over the creative and elgant patterns in your delicious coffee.


Latte art is a method of preparing coffee created by pouring steamed milk into a shot of espresso and creating a pattern or design on the surface of the latte. This is typically done by pouring the milk in a specific way, or by using a tool such as a toothpick or stencil to create the desired pattern.


Latte art is a mixture of both espresso and milk. The shot of espresso forms the crema of the coffee, and then steamed milk is added which is the microfoam that sits on the surface of the espresso, resulting in a pattern or design of the artwork.

HOW TO CREATE LATTE ART


Creating art requires an incredible amount of time and patience. Here are the basic steps involved in the creation of latte art:



Step 1: Start by brewing a shot of espresso and frothing your milk until it has a smooth and velvety texture.


Step 2: Pour the milk into the espresso shot slowly from a height of about 6 inches. This will create a base for your latte art.


Step 3: Once you have poured enough milk, start moving the pitcher in a circular motion to create a whirlpool effect. This will help to mix the milk and espresso together.


Step 4: When you're ready to create your design, pour the milk into the center of the espresso shot and then move the pitcher back and forth to create your desired pattern.


Step 5: Once you've finished your design, gently tap the bottom of the pitcher on the countertop to remove any bubbles.


Step 6: Finally, enjoy your latte art creation!
